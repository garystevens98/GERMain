About the EWRAP™

The Emergency Wireless Routing Access Point, EWRAP™, is a rugged multifunction router, specifically designed to provide a persistent, reliable local wireless connection between tablet and handheld computers, cameras and other Wi-­Fi enabled devices in and around emergency vehicles or carried in emergency kits. The unit provides a reliable persistent backhaul connection via a variety of technologies including 802.11a/b/g/n, 4.9GHz Public Safety, 802.16WiMAX, EV-­DO, UMTS, GSM-­EDGE and/or VSAT satellite.

Ports

Ports

Along the side of an EWRAP are a series of ethernet ports, as shown in FIGURE 1-4:

  1. Local and EWRAP Management Port: This port is used by a service technician for regular updates and maintenance configuration of the unit.
  2. Local Internet Access Port: This port is used to connect a local computer to the EWRAP device. A hub may also be connected to this port to connect more than one computer.
  3. Satellite/Alternate Backhaul: This port is used to connect the EWRAP to satellite or other backhaul services, if the connection is available and all other connection options are disabled.
