Entities

 

FIGURE 1-159: Entities

In an HC Standard® Matrix, or table, the rows represent "Entities" and the columns represent "Measures". Matrices can be managed using the tools found in the Entities category.

With HC Standard®, you are able to track and manage a wide range of information. The information could relate to a building, a hospital, a person, an incident or even a piece of equipment. Regardless of what you are tracking and managing, all of these things are called entities. Each entity has certain attributes and can be grouped together with other entities. As you begin using the system, you will see how helpful it is not to be locked into tracking and managing only certain items that are hard-coded into the application.

FIGURE 1-160 shows hospital entities. Each hospital listed in the "Name" column on the left is an entity.

FIGURE 1-160: Entities in a matrix

Measures are data elements that allow us, when taken together, to get a clear picture of the status of a given entity. For example, if we are monitoring the status of a "hospital" entity, useful measures might include supplies of blood and medications on hand in the hospital, and the current diversion status of the facility.
